Saudi Arabia disbands death penalty for minors

27 April 2020

Saudi Arabia stopped imposing death penalties on crimes commited under the age of 18

  Saudi Arabia has been under much criticism of Human Rights activist groups. They claim that Saudi Arabia is among the world's worst-performing countries when it comes to Human Rights.

   In an attempt to modernize its judicial system, Saudi Arabia has recently made two changes in the way it punishes convicted criminals.Last week, the country announced it would stop punishing criminals with beatings of a stick or whip. This week, Saudi Arabia has said it would disband the death penalty for juvenile delinquents. Yet, it remains unclear when these modernizations will actually be implemented.

  It is unclear whether the announcements relate to the death of a prominent activist last week.

Concerns regarding Human Rights

  As mentioned above, Saudi Arabia has received rather much criticism against its transgressions of human rights. Activist groups claim that the country systematically supresses freedom of expression - particularly with regard to women. For instance, in March 2019, authorities arrested a group of women's rights activists.

  Another criticism against Saudi Arabia's approach to freedom of expression made international news in 2018. It was held accountable for the murder of Jamal Khashoggi, a journalist who refuged in the Saudi consulate of Istanbul.

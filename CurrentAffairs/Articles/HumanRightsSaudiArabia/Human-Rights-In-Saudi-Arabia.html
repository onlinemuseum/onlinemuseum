Saudi Arabia disbands death penalty for minors

27 April 2020

Saudi Arabia stopped imposing death penalties on crimes commited under the age of 18

Introduction

 Today (27 April 2020) Saudi Arabia announced a further change of its judicial system. The statement came after Activist groups such as Amnesty International voiced resentment for letting a prominent activist die in prison

Modernizations

 Saudi Arabia seems to realize that the international community disdains its conduct: activist groups regard it as one of the world’s worst-performing countries when it comes to Human Rights.


So, in an attempt to modernize its judicial system, Saudi Arabia has recently announced it will make two changes regarding the way it punishes convicted criminals:

  • Last week, the country announced it would stop punishing criminals with beatings of a stick or whip.
  • This week, Saudi Arabia has said it would disband the death penalty for juvenile delinquents. Yet, it remains unclear when exactly these modernizations will be implemented.

It is unclear whether the announcements relate to the death of a prominent activist last week.

In 2019, Saudi Arabia granted women the right to drive

Concerns about Human Rights

 As mentioned above, Saudi Arabia has received rather much criticism against its transgressions of human rights. Activist groups claim that the country systematically suppresses freedom of expression — particularly with regard to women.

For instance, in March 2019, authorities arrested a group of women’s rights activists. Another criticism against Saudi Arabia’s approach to freedom of expression made international news in 2018. It was held accountable for the murder of Jamal Khashoggi, a journalist who refuged in the Saudi consulate of Istanbul.

Conclusion

 Since 2018, activists have become increasingly vocal about Human Rights in Saudi Arabia. It seems that Saudi Arabia is starting to listen to the calls by slowly liberalizing its judicial system and moving away from its old ‘Sharia’ ways.

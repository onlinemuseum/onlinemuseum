Ancient Egyptian Date Candy

Sweet | Snack

An Ancient Egyptian recipe for date candies. A recipe & history combined...

Introduction

  Dates are an indigenous fruit of Ancient Egypt. They grow in places all across the nile, and Egypt is one of the worlds largest exporter in the present day.

  In ancient times, the dates were a desired delectable too. They used the fruit to sweeten beer or make recipes like the one we learn today.

  Dates undoubtedly had a special meaning in Egyptian culture. This line from the book of death is an example of this cultural significance:

"In a clean place shall I sit on the ground. Beneath the foliage of a date palm from the godess Hathor"

~ Exerpt from a book of death.

Recipe

  • 35g ground walnuts
  • 1⁄4 PcApple
  • 2 Tsp Cinamon
  • 1 Tsp Cardamom seeds
  • Hand full of dates
  • 1 1⁄2 Tbs honey
  • 1 1⁄2 Tbs water

Instructions

  1. Grate the quarter of an apple
  2. Sprinkle first four ingredients over the entirety of a plate
  3. Heat the water and mix it with honey
  4. Baster the honey & water over a single date with a spoon or brush
  5. Roll the date over the plate with spices
  6. Repeat

Confusianism: a political theory engrained in virtue ethics

Published: 13 September 2022

How Confusius' political theory reflects his virtue ethics on the level of the individual.

Introduction

  In political theory there are many top-down views on statehood, Plato's philosopher king, Von Rochau's Realpolitik and Frederick the Great's Enlightened Absolutism are among the many classic concepts stressing that, for leaders, ends justify means [Machiavelli]. As we shall realize in this article, Confusius' political theory reflect his virtue ethics rather purely.

Virtue Ethics

  If one were to categorize Confusian ethics, Virtue ethics might be a suitable label. Because, similarly to Virtue Ethics, Confusius' philosophy revolves around moral virtue. In other words, when philosophising, the Confusianist school often considers the question 'what is morally right for a human to do?'.

Confusian ethics

  All we know about Confusius originates from the testimonies of his students. Based on these accounts, we know that Confusius sought to cultivate his pupils' characters to be morally good characters and to instill in them an innocent and pure way of thinking - and resulting action. On multiple occasions, Confusius proposed that people should be moral inside and out: their thinking must be pure, their (inter)actions must be good-hearted and their words must not be presumptuous or quasi-self-depracating (acting humble in a secret intent to glorify oneself).
